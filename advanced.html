📐 Definition

The Farey sequence Fₙ of order n is the sequence of completely reduced fractions between 0 and 1 with denominators ≤ n, arranged in increasing order.

🔢 Properties

Each sequence starts with 0/1 and ends with 1/1. The number of terms is 1 + Σφ(k) for k from 1 to n, where φ is Euler's totient function.

⚡ Mediant Property

If a/b and c/d are neighbors in Fₙ, then bc - ad = 1. Their mediant (a+c)/(b+d) first appears in F_{b+d}.

🎯 Applications

Farey sequences appear in number theory, rational approximation, the Riemann hypothesis, computer graphics (line drawing), and musical tuning systems.
